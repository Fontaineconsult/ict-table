Legal Overview Primary Purpose & Scope Prohibits discrimination in all aspects of employment (hiring, benefits, workplace systems).[1]
CA FEHA: Covers employers with ≥5 employees; broader “disability” definition (limits major life activity).[2][3] 		Requires state/local government programs, services, and digital content be equally accessible.[1]
§11135: Mirrors Title II for state‑funded activities; CSU ATI leverages this for systemwide accessibility in educational services.[4][5] 		Ensures private businesses (“public accommodations”) provide equal access to goods/services, including websites/apps.[1]
Unruh: ADA violations constitute state‑law violations; broader anti-discrimination in businesses.[6] 		Prohibits exclusion or denial of benefits in any program/activity receiving federal funds.[1]
§11135: Extends non‑discrimination to state‑funded programs; CSU ATI applies this to CSU campuses as state-funded entities.[4][5] 		Mandates accessible electronic and information technology for federal agencies.[1][7]
§7405: California state agencies must follow 508 standards; CSU ATI aligns procurement with these for state agencies.[5]

Grievance: File with DOJ or state agency; CSU ATI involves annual reporting; ADA Coordinator required for entities ≥50 employees.[1][14] 		DOJ enforcement, Unruh private suits; statutory damages per violation ($4k min).[1][6]
Grievance: Civil lawsuits under Unruh; no formal process, direct to court.[1][6] 		OCR/HHS complaints, corrective action, funding risk.[1]
Grievance: File with OCR or agency office; CSU ATI yearly reports track progress; §504 coordinator required for entities ≥15 employees.[1] 		Procurement holds, vendor CAPs; no private action.[7]
Grievance: Agency compliance review; CSU ATI uses status levels for auditing; complaints processed like §504.[15][7]

Protected: Citizens, students, constituents; CSU ATI emphasizes protection for CSU students, faculty, staff in public university settings.[1] 		Businesses & nonprofits open to the public; protects customers & visitors.[1]
Protected: Patrons, online users; includes virtual services.[1][6] 		Recipients of federal aid (schools, hospitals, agencies); protects participants.[1]
Protected: Students, patients, clients; CSU ATI focuses on CSU students and faculty in federally funded programs.[1] 		Federal agencies and vendors supplying ICT; protects employees & public users.[7]
Protected: Applicants, citizens; CSU ATI extends to public users of CSU ICT.[7][5]

Benchmark: WCAG 2.1 AA recommended; ADA violations trigger Unruh.[9][6] 		Reasonable modifications and auxiliary aids required unless undue hardship.[1]

Recent Updates FEHA SB 1137 (2025): broader “disability” definition; SB 1090 extends claims deadlines.[3] DOJ Title II Final Rule (Apr 2024): WCAG 2.1 AA mandate; enforcement trends include increased compliance focus post-deadlines.[10]; CSU ATI continues under EO 1111 (2018). DOJ Title III regs pending (no final rule as of July 2025); continue WCAG 2.1 AA; SB-84 for cure period stalled.[9] HHS §504 update (2024): WCAG 2.1 AA for health/education portals; deadlines May 2026 (≥15 employees).[12] 508 Refresh (2017) baseline; no refresh to WCAG 2.1/2.2 in 2025; watch for WCAG 2.1/2.2 alignment; CSU ATI promotes ongoing capacity building.[11]
Content Affected Accessibility Requirements (Digital) Internal HR/training platforms must support assistive tech (screen readers, keyboard nav), captions—tag PDFs, label forms; equal access via interactive process or alternatives.[3][8] Websites, apps, and documents must meet WCAG 2.1 AA: alt text, color contrast, ARIA, captions; CSU ATI supports through systemwide tools and training.[10][8] Customer‑facing digital services must follow WCAG patterns; dynamic content accessible; provide text alternatives; effective communication via auxiliary aids.[9][1] Digital content must be accessible or a timely alternate provided: e‑text, captions, Braille, audio descriptions; CSU ATI emphasizes for educational content.[1][12] ICT must conform to 508/WCAG 2.0 AA: keyboard operability, screen reader support, accessible UIs; CSU ATI provides procurement guidelines.[7][11]
Types of Digital Content Affected HR portals, applicant systems, performance dashboards, policy PDFs; online application systems, digital tools.[3] Government sites, e‑forms, PDF libraries, emergency alerts, mobile apps; CSU ATI includes university websites and apps.[10] E‑commerce sites, booking systems, event registration apps; websites as extensions of services.[9] Course materials, LMS modules, grant portals, telehealth apps; CSU ATI targets Canvas courses, multimedia.[12] Enterprise software, intranet apps, kiosks, digital signage; CSU ATI covers ICT purchases.[7]
Conformance Processes
Instructional Materials Employee training content (manuals, videos) provided in accessible formats as needed upon request (e.g., captioned videos, alternative manuals).[3]
CA FEHA: Includes workplace training accessibility as part of equal benefits.[3] 		Course syllabi, slides, videos, PDFs, LMS content must meet WCAG 2.1 AA by 2026; CSU ATI prioritizes documents, multimedia, Canvas courses for accessibility.[10][12]
§11135: California-funded schools follow same; equal access in education programs.[4] 		Private education providers must supply accessible materials; failure triggers ADA/Unruh claims; accessible examinations and courses required.[1] Provide reasonable modifications—Braille, audio, captions—for instructional content; collaborate with disability services; CSU ATI ensures timely access in federally funded CSU programs.[1][12] Federally-purchased educational software and e-learning modules must meet 508/WCAG standards; CSU ATI applies to ICT for instructional use.[7]
ICT Procurement Practices Employers must procure or adapt assistive technologies to accommodate employees.[3]
CA FEHA: AT procurement for smaller employers; consider accessibility to meet obligations.[3] 		Include accessibility in RFPs, review VPATs, enforce vendor remediation timelines; CSU ATI uses structured procurement process with accessibility reviews.[7][5] Private entities should embed accessibility requirements in purchasing policies to avoid liability; select accessible tech for customer-facing services.[9] Procure accessible products or plan accommodations to ensure program participants are not excluded; CSU ATI ensures compliance in federally funded purchases.[1] Section 508 requires federal agencies to buy 508‑conformant ICT; state agencies follow suit under §7405; CSU ATI reviews ICT for accessibility and security during procurement.[7][5]
